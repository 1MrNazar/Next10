1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, personalise your experience, and provide information to website owners.

Cookies can be persistent (they remain on your device until they expire or you delete them) or session cookies (they are deleted when you close your browser). They can be set by the website you are visiting ("first-party cookies") or by other organisations whose services are embedded on that website ("third-party cookies").

At this time, Next10 uses minimal cookies. We do not use advertising cookies, social media tracking cookies, or cookies that track you across other websites.

7. Local Storage and Similar Technologies

In addition to cookies, we use browser localStorage to remember your waitlist submission on your device. This prevents accidental duplicate form submissions and allows us to show you the success confirmation screen if you return to the Site.

Unlike cookies, localStorage data is not transmitted to our servers on every request - it stays exclusively on your device. You can clear it through your browser's developer tools or by clearing your site data in browser settings.

9. Do Not Track

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals to websites that you prefer not to be tracked. There is currently no legally enforceable or universally accepted standard for responding to DNT signals. We continue to monitor developments in this area and will update this policy if a standard is adopted.

When you first visit our Site, you may see a cookie consent notice. By continuing to use the Site, you consent to the use of cookies as described in this policy. You can withdraw consent at any time by adjusting your browser settings or using the Google Analytics opt-out tool described above.

Strictly necessary cookies and localStorage functionality do not require your consent as they are essential for the Site to operate as intended.
